Evolving Deception: Evolutionary Algorithms for Automated Red-Teaming of Instrumental Deception

Elias Schlie  ·  Supervisor: Dr. Emmanuel Keuleers
Tilburg University  ·  Department of Cognitive Science and Artificial Intelligence

Introduction

As LLMs are deployed in critical societal roles, we need scalable methods to detect when and how they deceive users.

Current red-teaming approaches rely on zero or multi-shot generation, requiring heavy human intervention to ensure breadth and realism of generated scenarios.

We propose an evolutionary approach that injects previously successful deceptive scenarios into the generation prompt, iteratively refining the attack population.

Research Question

To what extent does evolutionary optimization outperform zero and multi-shot generation in discovering realistic scenarios that induce instrumental deception in safety-aligned LLMs?

Background

  • Instrumental deception = goal-directed lies of commission, distinct from hallucination and sycophancy (Ren et al., 2026; Mahon, 2016)
  • Evaluation awareness: LLMs can detect testing and fake alignment (Greenblatt et al., 2024) — realistic scenarios are essential
  • Manual red-teaming is expensive and bottlenecked by human time (Hong et al., 2023)
  • Evolutionary methods maintain diverse attack populations and refine via fitness (Yu et al., 2024; Cheng et al., 2025)
  • Existing pipelines optimize for policy violations, not deception specifically (Jauhari et al., 2025)

Key Definitions

  • Instrumental Deception: Strategic, goal-directed lies — the model intentionally produces false statements to achieve an objective
  • Deception Success: Binary — did the target LLM commit a lie of commission? (1/0)
  • Realism: 1–7 Likert scale — would this scenario occur naturally?
  • Fitness = Realism × Deception Success — non-deceptive scenarios always score 0

Experimental Design

Three conditions compared with equivalent compute budgets:

Zero-shot Generator produces scenarios from task prompt alone. Unoptimized baseline.
Multi-shot Adds static, manually curated examples to the prompt. In-context learning baseline.
Evolutionary Fitness-proportional sampling from a growing population seeds each new generation.

Model: GLM-4.7-Flash for all roles (generator, target, judge). Cross-validated with Qwen3-VL-32B. Human annotation on 50-scenario subset.

Key Observations

📌
Multi-shot dominates early: Curated examples provide strong signal from the start, achieving 80% deception success
🧬
Evolution needs runway: With only 10 scenarios, the evolutionary approach has insufficient generations to build a strong population
🎯
Zero-shot fails completely: Without examples, the generator produces scenarios that safety-aligned models easily deflect
🔬
Hypothesis intact: Evolutionary advantage is expected at scale — the method's strength is iterative refinement over many generations

Next Steps

  • Run full-scale experiments across multiple topics with larger populations (100+ scenarios per condition)
  • Ensure equal compute budget across conditions for fair comparison
  • Cross-model validation with Qwen3-VL-32B as judge
  • Human annotation of 50-scenario subset for judge reliability
  • Statistical analysis: Chi-Square (success), Kruskal-Wallis (realism), Bonferroni-corrected post-hoc tests
