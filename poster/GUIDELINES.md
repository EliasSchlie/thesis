# Poster Presentation Guidelines

*Last edit: August 21, 2025*

In this graded assignment (pass/fail), you will present your thesis proposal and progress so far with a scientific poster. Posters are a means of demonstrating your projects to scientific staff members and other students in an exhibition-like setting to get feedback. Optional: you could bring a demo or show a video on your laptop (if applicable).

## Step-by-Step Instructions

### 1. Create Your Poster

- **Format:** A0 size (ideally in portrait format).
- **Software:** Free to use your own software of choice.
- **Content:** Include the essential parts of your thesis proposal. Ideally, a poster should be complete and informative even without a presenter present.
- **Layout:** Utilize good poster design principles regarding font size, use of bullets, amount of text, flow, and color.
- **Visuals:** Incorporate text, tables, graphs, and graphics appropriately to present your project clearly.

### 2. Tailor Your Content

- **Research Variability:** Different research questions or experiments will require different presentation focuses. For instance, if data collection is not involved, the focus might be on exploratory data analysis and the presentation of summary tables for such data.
- **Clarity:** Ensure any tables or graphs included are clear and directly related to your project.

### 3. Print Your Poster

You are yourself responsible for printing your poster. Poster printing can be done in local copy shops, on campus (STUDIO: printing and design) or online (e.g., drukwerkdeal.nl). Make sure you send your poster to the printer in time, because printing typically takes a full working day (or longer if ordered online).

### 4. Poster Presentation Session

The poster presentation is the second opportunity for students to present their project. Students present their projects to scientific staff members and other students in an exhibition-like setting. During the poster session students will present their poster to their supervisor or other assessor for grading by giving a short oral presentation (about 5 minutes) of their poster followed by a question and answering session. If applicable, students should process the feedback given by the examiner, other lecturers and peers in the final deliverables handed at the end of the project. The poster presentation constitutes an instance of summative assessment and is evaluated by a pass/fail by the examiner.

### 5. Prepare for the Presentation

- **Time:** Rehearse your presentation to ensure you can walk the audience through within the approximately 5-minute timeframe.
- **Questions:** Be ready to answer questions from your peers, supervisor, and other faculty members.

### 6. Submit Your Poster

- **Format:** Upload your poster in PDF format in the dedicated Canvas Assignment.

## Tips for an Effective Poster

- **Conciseness:** Avoid putting too much text on your poster. Focus on the main takeaways.
- **Visuals:** Use images and graphs to convey your message. Posters should have a strong visual communication element.
- **Grading rubric:** Refer to the rubric to ensure the poster and the presentation satisfy all requirements.
